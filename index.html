Johannesburg — South Africans who complete their BCom qualification this year will be required to complete one year of compulsory national economic service before receiving their degrees. The new measure forms part of the government’s aggressive Economic Turnaround Strategy.

The compulsory service, which begins in January 2027, will see graduates deployed into a national Economic Task Force aimed at driving growth, improving public finances, and supporting key state institutions.

Director of the Solidarity Movement, Werner Human, has already vowed to challenge the decision in the highest court. “Holding young graduates hostage because of the government’s failed economic policies is unfair and unconstitutional. Real growth comes from economic freedom, not forced labour,” he said.

Graduates will reportedly report to the South African Reserve Bank on 2 January 2027 for induction and placement. Exact roles remain unclear, but sources indicate positions in finance, administration, data analysis, and public sector support.

Compensation is expected to be R4,000 per month, with state-provided accommodation. Students who refuse to serve will be barred from claiming their qualifications for a minimum of five years.

The initiative has already been dubbed the “Godongwana Year,” in reference to Finance Minister Enoch Godongwana, and is being compared to the compulsory community service required of medical graduates.
